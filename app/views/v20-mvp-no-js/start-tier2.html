Medical professionals with a Tier 2 (General) visa

You'll be eligible to apply for a refund if you both:

  • have a Tier 2 (General) visa
  • work in an eligible job

If you work in an eligible job but do not have a Tier 2 visa, you will need to apply for a refund the same way as other roles in the health and care sector.

Eligible jobs

You can get a refund if you're a:

  • biological scientist
  • biochemist
  • dental practitioner
  • doctor
  • midwife
  • medical radiographer
  • nurse
  • ophthalmic optician
  • paramedic
  • pharmacist
  • physiotherapist
  • physical scientist
  • podiatrist
  • psychologist
  • social worker
  • speech and language therapist
  • therapy professional

What you will get

You will get a full or partial refund, depending on when you paid the immigration health surcharge (IHS).

If you paid the IHS on or after 31 March 2020, you will get a full refund.

If you paid the IHS before 31 March 2020, you will get a partial refund. You will be refunded for any periods of 6 months that your IHS payment covered after 31 March 2020.

The money will be paid back to the account or card you used to pay the IHS.

Dependants

If you are eligible, your dependants can also get a refund.


How to apply

To apply, you need to contact UK Visas and Immigration (UKVI). You need to include:

  • the IHS reference numbers for you and any dependants
  • the date you and any dependants paid the IHS
  • your Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS) number
  • your employer's name

You'll be charged for calling UKVI - find out more about call charges.

If you have not got your refund

If you have applied for a refund but have not yet got it yet, contact UKVI.
