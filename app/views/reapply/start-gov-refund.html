! Warning Are you applying for a refund or reimbersement? Please read the following to make sure you know what you are applying for

What is a Reimbursement?

For the purpose of this application (or section) ‘reimbursement’ means a partial repayment of a six-month block of the total amount the applicant paid when they paid the Immigration Health Surcharge. Providing the applicant is, not eligible for a Health and Care visa, but works in the Health or Care sector and meets all other eligibility criteria.

Apply for an IHS reimbursement if you work in health and social care.

What is a Refund?

For the purpose of this application (or section) ‘refund’ means a full repayment of the total amount the applicant paid when they paid the Immigration Health Surcharge. Providing the applicant is eligible for a Health and Care visa please continue to claim a refund.

Refunds

You'll get a full immigration health surcharge (IHS) refund if:

  • you paid twice
  • your visa application is refused
  • you withdrew your visa application

You'll get a partial IHS refund if your visa application's successful but:

  • you get less time on your visa than you asked for
  • any dependants on your visa application are refused

If you are due a full or partial refund for these reasons, you do not have to do anything yet to get it. It will be automatically paid to the account or card you paid with.

You will not get a refund if:

  • your visa application is successful but you do not come to the UK
  • you leave the UK before your visa ends, for example to make a new application
  • you're told to leave the UK before your visa expires
  • you're applying for indefinite leave to remain

If you're changing to the Tier 2 Health and Care vise, you will get a full refund of your IHS from 31 March 2020. You will not get a refund if you're changing to any other visa.

If you work in health and care

Health and care workers can apply for a refund of any IHS they paid to cover the period from 31 March 2020.

You must have worked in an eligible health or care role for at least 6 months since 31 March 2020, working for an average of at least 16 hours a week.

If you meet these conditions, your dependants are also entitiled to a refund.

What you'll get

Each time you apply, you will get 6 months' worth of the IHS you have paid, up to a maximum of £200. You will need to apply every 6 months.

You can only get a refund of the IHS you've paid to cover your healthcare from 31 March 2020 onwards.

If you're eligible for the Tier 2 Health and Care visa

You and your dependants do not normally need to pay the IHS if you either

  • have a Tier 2 Health and Care visa
  • would have been eligible for a Tier 2 Health and Care visa on or after 4 August 2020

If you paid the IHS on or after 31 March 2020, you should automatically get full refund.

If you're eligible for a Tier 2 Health and Care visa and have not yet received a refund, email the IHS refunds team. In your mail include:

  • name
  • sponsor's name
  • Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS) number
  • IHS number

Do not send your bank details. All refunds will be paid to the account or card you paid with.

How long it takes

You usually get your refund within 6 weeks of getting a decision on your visa application. It can take longer if you appeal or ask for an administative review after your visa application is refused.

If you appeal or ask for an administative review

If you applied from:

  • inside the UK - you'll get your refund up to 6 weeeks after your appeal or administrtive review is dismissed
  • outside the UK - you'll get your refund your to 6 weeks after your visa application is refused

You'll have to repay the IHS if your appeal or administative review is successful and you've already got your IHS refund.

You might have to repay a different amount if:

  • the lenght of your stay changes
  • you get less time on yopur visa than you asked for

Contact UK Visas and Immigration (UKVI) if your refund is not paid within 6 weeks
