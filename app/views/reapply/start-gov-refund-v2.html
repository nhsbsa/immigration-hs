Refunds

Automatic refunds

You’ll get a full immigration health surcharge (IHS) refund if:

  • you paid twice
  • your visa application is refused
  • you withdraw your visa application

You’ll get a partial IHS refund if your visa application’s successful but:

  • you get less time on your visa than you asked for
  • any dependants on your visa application are refused

If you are due a full or partial refund for these reasons, you do not have to do anything to get it. It will be automatically paid to the account or card you paid with.

You will not get a refund if:

  • your visa application is succesful, but you do not come to the UK
  • you leave the UK before your visa ends, for example to make a new application
  • you’re told to leave the UK before your visa expires
  • you’re applying for indefinite leave to remain

If you’re changing to the Tier 2 Health and Care visa, you will get a full refund of your IHS from 31 March 2020. You will not get a refund if you’re changing to any other visa.

There are three refund categories depending on your working circumstances and class of visa. Please read the following to determine which category of refund you should apply for.

You and your dependants do not normally need to pay the IHS if you either:

  • have a Tier 2 Health and Care visa
  • would have been eligible for a Tier 2 Health and Care visa on or after 4 August 2020

If you paid on or after 31 March 2020, you should automatically get a full refund. If you paid before 31 March 2020 continue to the next category.


Tier 2 visa related refunds

You and your dependents may be entitled to a refund if you have a Tier 2 (General) Visa if you the following criteria:

  • are working in health and social care
  • you paid the surcharge before 31 March 2020 and it covers a period of at least 6 months (If you paid after 31 March 2020, you should automatically get a full refund, see Automatic Refunds above)
  • work in an eligible health or care role. For example: nurse, midwife, paramedic. (If you are not working in an eligible role please go to IHS Reimbursement Refunds below)

To apply for this refund send an mail to IHSrefunds@homeoffice.gov.uk with the following information:

  • name
  • sponsor's name
  • Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS) number
  • IHS number

Do not send your bank details. All refunds will be paid to the account or card you paid with.

If you are not eligible for automatic refunds or UKVI visa related refunds continue to IHS reimbursements.


IHS Reimbursements

You and your dependents may be entitled to a reimbursement of the immigration health surcharge you have paid if you meet the following criteria:

  • currently working or have previously worked in a health and social care sector role
  • for 6 months or more
  • for an average of 16 hours per week
  • on or after 31 March 2020

Apply for an IHS reimbursement if you work in health and care.


How long it takes

You usually get your refund within 6 weeks of getting a decision on your visa application. It can take longer if you appeal or ask for an administrative review after your visa application is refused.

If you appeal or ask for an administrative review

NOTE: This relates to Automatic refunds and Tier 2 related refunds, and NOT IHS Reimbursements

If you applied from:

  • inside the UK - you'll get your refund up to 6 weeks after your appeal or administrative review is dismissed
  • outside the UK - you'll get your refund up to 6 weeks after your visa application is refused

You'll have to repay the IHS if your appeal or administrative review is successful and you've already got your IHS refund.

You might have to repay a different amount if:

  • the length of your stay changes
  • you get less time on your visa than you asked for

Contact UK Visas and Immigration (UKVI) if your refund is not paid within 6 weeks.
