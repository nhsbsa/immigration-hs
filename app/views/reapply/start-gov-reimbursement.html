Apply for an immigration health surcharge reimbursement if you work in health and social care

You may be able to get a reimbursement if you paid the immigration health surcharge (IHS) and you work in the health and care sector. The health and care sector included hospitals, GP practices, care homes and community healthcare facilities

There is a different process if you have a Tier 2 (Health and Care) visa and you're applying for a refund. Apply for an IHS refund if you work in health and social care.

Who can apply

To be eligible for using this service, the applicant must be working or have previously worked in a health and social care sector job:

  • for 6 months or more
  • for an average of 16 hours per week
  • on or after 31 March 2020

Applicants can only claim a reimbursement if they have paid a surcharge whilst on their current vise.

A person who is eligible to use this service will be the applicant. The applicant’s dependants will also be automatically eligible. A main applicant will be able to add dependants using this service, but dependants cannot add a main applicant.

Eligibility

You can apply for the reimbursement if you work in an eligible role in the health or care sector, including:

  • direct patient care, for example as a health care assistant or social care worker
  • administartive or leadership, for example as a manager in a care home, or a hospital receptionist
  • facilities management, for example as cleaner, caterer or courier in a hospital

You must also work for an eligible organisation, such as:

  • the NHS
  • the Care Quality Commission (CQC)
  • Health Education England (HEE)
  • Medicines and Healthcare products Regulatory Agency (MHRA)
  • Public Health England (PHE)
  • the National Institute for Health and Care Excellence (NICE)

Read the full guidance about which jobs and organisations are eligible

You must have worked in your role for at least 6 months, for an average of at least 16 hours a week.

The 6 month can include paid leave, such as maternity or stutory sick leave. You will not be eligible if you were unemployed or had unpaid leave for more than 28 days during the 6 months.

You can only get a reimbursement of the IHS you've paid that covers a 6-month period from 31 March 2020 onwards.

Applying for your dependants

If you're eligible, your dependants can also get a reimbursement.

Your dependants cannot apply for themselves - you must include them in your application.

How to apply

To use this service, the applicant must apply. If you are using the service on behalf of an applicant, use their details. Claims made will be for a period of 6 months.

You'll need the applicants:

  • full name as it appears on their passport
  • immigration health surcharge number
  • payslips to cover each month of the 6-month period of the claim - these can be uploaded as copies using this service
  • payment method that the immigration health surcharge was paid from and access to it
  • dependants’ details, if applicable - you'll need their names and immigration health surcharge numbers

Apply online

To apply for the reimbursement you'll need:

  • your IHS number (this is a 12-digit number
  • your National Insurance number
  • your email address
  • the name of your employer
  • copies of your payslips for the last 6 months - you can upload them as either a scan or a photo
If you're applying for your dependants, you'll also need their IHS number, National Insurance number and email address.

If your application is successful, you'll be sent the reimbursement withing 6 weeks.

What you'll get

This service is designed for applicants who hold visas in the UK, to be able to make a claim to get a reimbursement for any immigration health surcharges they have paid whilst on their current visa.

Reimbursements are not paid as a single payment. They're paid in 6-month instalments, in arrears, covering the period from 31 March 2020. The reimbursement amount will be 6-months' worth of what the applicant has paid for their immigration health surcharge.

Each time you apply, you will get 6 months' woth of IHS you've already paid, up to a maximum of £200. You'll need to apply every 6 months to get reimbursed.

You can only get a reimbursement of the IHS you've paid to cover your healthcare from 31 March 2020 onwards and a reimbursement claim cannot be made for any period before 31 March 2020.

How we'll reimburse you

The applicant will get their reimbursement through the same payment method they used to pay the IHS.

Making a claim takes around 30 minutes.
