Apply for an immigration health surcharge reimbursement if you work in health and social care

You may be able to get a reimbursement if you paid the immigration health surcharge (IHS) and you work in the health and care sector. The health and care sector included hospitals, GP practices, care homes and community healthcare facilities.

There is a different process if you have a Tier 2 (Health and Care) visa and you're applying for a refund.

An applicant is entitled to claim reimbursement of the IHS if that visa is not a Tier 2 (General) visa, and if they have provided the required evidence to demonstrate they have completed the eligible hours of eligible work.

Apply for an IHS refund if you work in health and social care.

Who can apply

You can use this service if you're the applicant. If you're using this service on behalf of the applicant, use the applicant's details.

To be eligible to use this service, the applicant must be working or have previously worked in a health and social care sector job:

  • for 6 months or more, for an average of 16 hours per week
  • on or after 31 March 2020, in order to get a reimbursement, any charges before this date cannot be reimbursed
  • and paid a surcharge whilst on their current visa

Applicants will not be eligible if they were unemployed or had unpaid leave for more than 28 days during the 6 months. However, the 6 months can include paid leave, such as maternity or statutory sick leave.

The applicant's dependants will also be automatically eligible. A main applicant will be able to add dependants using this service, but dependants cannot add a main applicant.

Applicants can apply for the reimbursement if they work in an eligible role in the health or care sector, including:

  • direct patient care, for example, as a health care assistant or social care worker
  • administrative or leadership, for example, as a manager in a care home, or a hospital receptionist
  • facilities management, for example, as a cleaner, caterer or courier in a hospital

You must also work for an eligible organisation, such as:

  • the NHS
  • the Care Quality Commission (CQC)
  • Health Education England (HEE)
  • Medicines and Healthcare products Regulatory Agency (MHRA)
  • Public Health England (PHE)
  • the National Institute for Health and Care Excellence (NICE)

View a list of jobs and organisations which are eligible

How to apply

You'll need the applicants:

  • full name as it appears on their passport
  • immigration health surcharge number
  • payslips to cover each month of the 6-month period of the claim - these can be uploaded as copies using this service
  • payment method that the immigration health surcharge was paid from and access to it
  • dependants' details, if applicable - you'll need their names and immigration health surcharge numbers

Claims made will be for a period of 6 months. After that, the applicant may need to claim again.

How we'll reimburse you

We'll reimburse the applicant 6-months' worth of payments in arrears. Reimbursement claims cannot be made for any period before 31 March 2020.

The reimbursement amount will be 6-months' worth of what the applicant has paid for their immigration health surcharge.

The applicant will get their reimbursement as one single payment through the same payment method they used to pay the IHS.

Each time the applicant applies, they'll get 6 months' worth of the IHS they've already paid. They may need to apply every 6 months to get reimbursed.


Making a claim takes around 30 minutes.

If you do not enter any information for more than 30 minutes, your application will time out and you'll have to start again.
