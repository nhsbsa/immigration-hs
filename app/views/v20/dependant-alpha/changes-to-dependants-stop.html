Changes to the online immigration health surcharge reimbursement application

The changes we explain on this page were made in March 2026.

Changes to our duplicate and overlap checks

To help reduce the number of duplicate and overlapping claims received, we have improved the verification checks to prevent any claims with matching claim dates, IHS numbers or National Insurance numbers.

This will reduce the number of rejections and potential over payments.

This means that people who made a duplicate or overlapping claim to apply for their dependant with a different claim period will be unable to do so.

Dependants with different visa start or claim period dates

You can only apply for an immigration healthcare surcharge (IHS) in full 6-month periods.

If the depenedant's visa started after the main applicant's, their reimbursement period may not line up at first.

To avoid problems with future clams, you may need to leave your dependant off your first claim and then add them from your next claim instead.

Example

Main applicant claim period: 1 January 2022 to 30 June 2022

Dependant claim period: 1 March 2022 to 31 August 2022

Because your dependant's visa started later, their first full 6-month period does not match your January to June claim.

What you need to do

If the dependant's visa started later than the main applicant you must:

  1. Submit your first claim without your dependant - this will cover the main applicants 6 month period only.
  2. Add the dependant to the next claim - from the next claim onwards, the claim periods will be aligned and you can apply as normal going forward.
