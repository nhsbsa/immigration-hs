The applicant may be able to get money back if they paid the immigration health surcharge (IHS) and they work in health and social care.

There are 2 different ways to get money back, depending on the applicant’s visa and job.

The applicant can apply for a refund if they’re a health and care professional and have a Tier 2 (General) visa or Tier 2 (General) health and care visa.

The applicant can apply for a reimbursement if they’re a:

  • Tier 2 (General right to work) visa holder
  • Tier 4 (Student) visa holder who is working in the health and social care sector alongside their studies
  • Tier 5 youth mobility scheme visa holder
  • dependant of a UK national or another migrant

This list is not exhaustive.
