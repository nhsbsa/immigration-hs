Other roles in health and care settings

You can apply for a refund ('reimbursement') online if you:

  • have paid the immigration health surcharge (IHS)
  • work for an eligible company or organisation, for example, the NHS
  • have worked in your role for at least 6 months, for an average of at least 16 hours a week

The 6 months can include paid leave, such as maternity or statutory sick leave.

You will not be eligible if you were unemployed or had unpaid leave for more than 28 days during the 6 months.

You can also apply this way if you are a medical professional but do not have a Tier 2 visa.

Eligible organisations

Examples of eligible organisations include:

  • the NHS
  • the Care Quality Commission (CQC)
  • National Institute for Health and Care Excellence

Check 'Annex A' of our detailed guidance for more details about eligible organisations.

Dependants

Your dependants can also get a refund if you are eligible. You need to include them in your application.

When to apply and what you will get

You can only apply if you have worked in an eligible role for at least 6 months.

You can apply for a refund for one 6-month period at a time.

The period you apply for must start on or after 31 March 2020. If you paid the IHS before then, you can still apply but you'll only be reimbursed for the time after 31 March 2020.

Example

If you paid the IHS on 20 December 2019, you could get money back for the period from 31 March 2020 to 30 September 2020.

You should enter 31 March 2020 as the start date for your claim because you could not get money back for the time before that.

If your application is successful, you will be sent the money within 6 weeks.

The money will be sent to the account or card you used to pay the IHS. If a third party paid your IHS, the money will go to their account
