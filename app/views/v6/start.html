Apply for your immigration health surcharge reimbursement

What you'll get

This service is designed for applicants who hold visas in the UK, to be able to make a claim to get a reimbursement for any immigration health surcharges they have paid whilst on their current visa.

Reimbursements are processed in 6-month instalments, in arrears, covering the period from 31 March 2020. The maximum amount to be reimbursed is £200 per person.

The applicant will need to reapply after every 6 months to continue getting reimbursed.

A reimbursement claim cannot be made for any period before 31 March 2020.

Who can apply

To be eligible for using this service, the applicant must be working or have previously worked in a health and social care sector job:

  • for 6 months or more
  • for an average of 16 hours per week
  • on or after 31 March 2020

Applicants can only claim a reimbursement if they have paid a surcharge whilst on their current visa.

A person who is eligible to use this service will be the applicant. The applicant’s dependants will also be automatically eligible. A main applicant will be able to add dependants using this service, but dependants cannot add a main applicant.

How to apply

To use this service, the applicant must apply. If you are using the service on behalf of an applicant, use their details. Claims made will be for a period of 6 months.

You’ll need the applicant’s:

  • full name as it appears on their passport
  • immigration health surcharge number
  • payslips to cover each month of the 6-month period of the claim - these can be uploaded as copies using this service
  • payment method that the immigration health surcharge was paid from and access to it
  • dependants’ details, if applicable - you'll need their names and immigration health surcharge numbers

How we’ll reimburse you

The applicant will get their reimbursement through the same payment method they used to pay the IHS.

Making a claim takes around 30 minutes.
