You may be able to get a reimbursement of part of your Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS) back if you’re working or have worked in the Health or Social Care sector.

Eligibility criteria

To be eligible to apply, you must have:

  • paid an IHS, or someone must have paid the IHS on your behalf
  • worked in the Health or Social Care sector for at least 6 months after 31 March 2020, for at least an average of 16 hours per week

If you’re eligible and you have a partner and / or dependents who have also paid an IHS, they’ll also be eligible.

How to apply for an IHS reimbursement

You can use our online service.

We’ll refer to “the applicant” throughout the online service. This will be yourself if you’re making the claim for an IHS you have paid. If you’re using this service on behalf of someone else who has paid an IHS, “the applicant” will be the person you’re using the service on behalf of.

You’ll need:

  • the applicant’s personal details, including full name, IHS reference number, National Insurance number and contact details
  • the applicant’s employment history over the last 6 months
  • details of any dependants who are also eligible

You’ll also be asked to provide evidence that the applicant hasin the Health and Care Sector during the qualifying period – you’ll be able to upload payslips to support this.

Before you start

You can also register by post.

The online service is also available in Welsh (Cymraeg).

You can’t register for this service if you’re in the UK illegally.
