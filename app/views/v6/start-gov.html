Apply for your immigration health surcharge reimbursement

You may be able to get a reimbursement of part of your Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS) back if you're working or have worked in the Health or Social Care sector.

Eligibility criteria

To be eligible to apply, you must have:

  • paid an IHS, or someone must have paid the IHS on your behalf
  • worked in the Health or Social Care sector for at least 6 months after 31 March 2020, for at least an average of 16 hours per week

Your eligibility will also depend on what job you do. You can view a list of employers that are covered. This is not a complete list. If your employer is not listed, we'll ask you for more information within this service.

If you're eligible and you have a partner and / or dependants who have also paid an IHS, they'll also be eligible. You'll need to add each independant individually when using this service.

How to apply for an IHS reimbursement

You can use our online service.

We'll refer to “the applicant” throughout this online service. This will be yourself if you're making the claim for an IHS you have paid. If you're using this service on behalf of someone else who has paid an IHS, “the applicant” will be the person you're using the service on behalf of.

Apply for your IHS reimbursement

You can also make a claim by:

  • Telephone: 0300 330 7693
  • From outside the UK: +44 191 283 8937
  • Monday to Friday, 8am to 6pm and Saturday, 9am to 3pm.

Find out about call charges

If making a claim by phone, we'll ask you to send the applicant's payslips to us as evidence that they've worked in the Health and Care Sector. Payslips sent must cover the 6-month period of the claim and also include the claim reference number.

If the applicant was off work during the 6-month period of the claim

The applicant may still be eligible because:

  • statutory entitlement to paid leave, including time spent on statutory sick pay, counts towards 6 months' continuous service - this also includes leave such as maternity, paternity and adoption leave
  • they may be working or have worked for more than one employer, or changed employer
  • breaks from employment or unpaid leave will count towards 6 months' continuous service, where that break or unpaid leave is no longer than 28 days in total

The applicant will not be reimbursed for any gaps in employment that's more than the allowed period of 28 days.
