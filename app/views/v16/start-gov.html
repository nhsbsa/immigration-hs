The applicant may be able to get money back if they paid the immigration health surcharge (IHS) and they work in health and social care.

There are 2 different ways to get money back, depending on the applicant's visa and job.

The applicant can apply for a refund if they, either:

  • work in health and care and have a Skilled Worker visa, in an eligible role
  • are the dependant of someone who works in health and care, who has a Skilled Worker visa, in an eligible role

Skilled Worker is the new name for the Tier 2 (General) visa.

View the list of eligible roles
List of roles:
  • nurses
  • midwives
  • social workers
  • paramedics
  • doctor
  • psychologists
  • pharmacists
  • ophthalmic opticians
  • dental practitioners
  • medical radiographers
  • podiatrists
  • health professionals not elsewhere classified
  • physiotherapists
  • occupational therapists
  • speech and language therapists
  • therapy professionals not elsewhere classified
  • biological scientists and biochemists
  • physical scientists

If you're a Tier 5 Government Authorised Exchange Medical Training Initiative (GAE MTI) visa holder, you do not need to apply for a refund of your IHS. The Tier 5 GAE MTI was previously known as a Tier 5 Medical Training Initiative (MTI).

Your refund will be processed automatically followed by notification of this from the Academy of Medical Royal Colleges.

Applicants on all other visa types can apply for a reimbursement if they've paid the Immigration Health Surcharge, are on a visa that allows them to work, and they work in Health and Social Care.
